==============================================================================
  PHISHING EMAIL SCREENSHOT DETECTOR — SAMPLE TEST GUIDE
==============================================================================

This guide shows you how to create test images to verify the app is working
correctly. You don't need to have real phishing emails.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
METHOD 1: TYPE A FAKE EMAIL AND SCREENSHOT IT
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Step 1: Open Notepad (Windows) or TextEdit (macOS).
Step 2: Type one of the test email samples below.
Step 3: Take a screenshot of the Notepad window.
        Windows: Press  Win + Shift + S  → select the area → save as PNG.
        macOS:   Press  Cmd + Shift + 4  → drag to select → saved to Desktop.
Step 4: Upload the screenshot to the app and click "Analyze Email".

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TEST SAMPLE 1 — EXPECTED RESULT: 🚨 PHISHING
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Paste the following into Notepad, screenshot it, and upload:

From: support@paypall-secure.com
To: you@example.com
Subject: URGENT: Your PayPal Account Has Been Suspended

Dear Customer,

URGENT: We have detected suspicious activity on your PayPal account.
Your account has been suspended due to unauthorized access.

You must verify your account immediately or your account will be terminated.

Click here to verify your account:
http://192.168.1.55/paypal-login/verify?id=abc123

Failure to respond within 24 hours will result in permanent account closure.

Download the attached file to complete verification: verify_account.exe

ACTION REQUIRED. Do not ignore this email.

PayPal Security Team
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

What the detector should find:
  ✓ Phishing Keywords: "verify your account", "dear customer",
                       "account has been suspended", "click here",
                       "account will be terminated"
  ✓ Suspicious URL: IP address-based URL (192.168.x.x)
  ✓ IP Address in URL: detected
  ✓ Urgency Language: "respond immediately", "failure to respond",
                      "your account has been suspended"
  ✓ Suspicious Attachment: .exe extension
  ✓ Sender-Domain Mismatch: claims to be PayPal, sent from paypall-secure.com
  ✓ ALL-CAPS: URGENT, ACTION REQUIRED

Expected score: ~35+ / 51
Expected classification: 🚨 PHISHING

METHOD 2: USE A REAL PHISHING EXAMPLE (SAFELY)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

If you have received a suspected phishing email in your inbox:

1. DO NOT click any links inside the email.
2. Open the email in your email client (Gmail, Outlook, etc.).
3. Take a screenshot of just the email body and header area.
4. Upload the screenshot to this app.

WARNING: Never forward phishing emails or open attachments.
         Only screenshot and analyse the visible text.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
METHOD 3: PHISHING EMAIL DATABASES FOR TESTING
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

There are publicly available phishing email datasets for research and testing:

1. PhishTank (https://www.phishtank.com) — Verified phishing URL database.
   You can create a fake email screenshot mentioning one of their listed URLs.

2. The Enron Email Dataset — Public domain email corpus.
   Available at: https://www.cs.cmu.edu/~./enron/

3. APWG eCrime Reports — https://apwg.org/resources/apwg-reports/
   Contains analysis of real phishing campaigns.

Note: Always handle phishing samples in a safe, isolated environment.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TIPS FOR BEST OCR RESULTS
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

• Use PNG format — higher quality than JPG for text.
• Take screenshots at 100% zoom — don't shrink the window.
• Avoid dark mode emails — light background + dark text gives best OCR accuracy.
• Crop tightly around the email text — removes UI chrome and improves focus.
• If OCR produces garbled text, try increasing your display resolution or
  system DPI settings before taking the screenshot.
